Dina Titus

Democratic Candidate for Nevada

Dina Titus (Democratic Party) is a member of the U.S. House, representing Nevada's 1st Congressional District. She assumed office on January 3, 2013. Her current term ends on January 3, 2021. Titus (Democratic Party) is running for re-election to the U.S. House to represent Nevada's 1st Congressional District. She is on the ballot in the general election on November 3, 2020.

Joyce Bentley

Republican Candidate for Nevada

Joyce Bentley (Republican Party) is running for election to the U.S. House to represent Nevada's 1st Congressional District. She is on the ballot in the general election on November 3, 2020. She advanced from the Republican primary on June 9, 2020. Bentley's professional experience includes working as a banker, realtor, and small business owner.

Healthcare

There is currently a shortage of healthcare professionals in Nevada. This severely limits the primary care coverage provided to Nevadans. Currently, there are only about 170 licensed Allopathic Physicians per 100,00 people living in Nevada. In addition, insurance rates are getting higher, which is obviously problematic for low-income families.

Tidus's Stance

Dina Titus strives to uphold the already established Affordable Care Act of Nevada. This will continue to bring affordable healthcare to Nevada residents.

Bentley's Stance

Bentley agrees with President Trump's insist for legislation H.R. 878 the “Right to Try Act” of 2017 be enacted, that would allow terminally ill patients access to new innovative medical procedures and medicines that have not yet been approved by the FDA.

Gun Rights

In Nevada, a person is killed by a firearm approximately every 20 hours. This is especially true in Las Vegas, nicknamed “Sin City”. In 2017, Las Vegas, a mass shooting at Mandalay Bay killed 61 people, and injured over 800 people. Nevada is one of the 21 states where gun deaths exceed motor vehicle-related deaths. Guns have become a serious concern among all Americans.

Tidus's Stance

"From large cities to rural communities, nowhere is immune from this epidemic" "We need action and we need leadership that will restore the assault weapons ban," she says. This year more children have died at school than have service members on the battlefield, she adds. "Instead of promoting real solutions, he is pushing to arm teachers for the benefit of gun manufacturers" "Republican leaders and the gun lobby cannot ignore these tragedies any longer," Titus says of President Trump. "Every day our friends, family members, and neighbors are dying from preventable gun violence"

Bentley's Stance

“On the heels of the 1st American Revolution the fore fathers of this great nation did not establish the 2nd amendment for hunting or sport; it was established specifically, against a tyrannical government that could possibly try to rise against the American people.”

COVID-19

Current pandemic involving a virus which is somewhat deadly to people with weak immune systems and has spread rapidly throughout Nevada, among other places. Current confirmed cases is around 68,000 and is still growing because of people who are not taking proper precautions against the virus.

Tidus's Stance

Dina Titus works in Washington, D.C. to ensure that every level of government has the resources needed to keep the public safe. The first emergency “funding bill” that they passed has provided resources to public health facilities, funding to develop a vaccine, and loans for small businesses. Then they passed the “Families First Coronavirus Response Act” to make sure that all coronavirus tests are free and expand unemployment benefits, nutritional assistance and leave. Now she’s working with my colleagues to pass a long-term recovery package.

Bentley's Stance

Bentley has been inactive since may of this year and statements from her on COVID-19 could not be found.
